About.

I consider myself a talented designer and a knowledgeable developer of digital experiences. I sincerely enjoy learning about the web industry and delight in creating pleasant and accessible experiences for all to enjoy. My inspirations are drawn from a variety of sources and influences.

Process

My process is ever evolving and with each project I make effort to learn something new.

Throughout my career, I’ve compiled my process to these main steps: Learn, Prototyping, Design, Deliver and Learn Some More.

Learning

With every project I learn as much as possible about the client requirements. Things like demographics, purpose and function, goals and flow are items I like to learn about as early in the project as possible. High-level concepts tend to form the base of the project and how the project materializes so I find it most efficient to learn these items early on.

I perform as much research as possible in tandem with the learning stage, outlining any objectives and solutions that may help achieve our goal. Objectives: Sitemaps, Map User Flow, User Persona

Wireframing and Prototyping

Wireframing for me starts with quick and simple drawings. I create numerous thumbnails in the attempt to build out multiple solutions to singular objective. I find the more I sketch, the better the end results are. It allows for exploration of solutions to a main objective without committing to anything specific.

My prototypes are built in a variety of tools; I’m well-versed in a number of programs. A list of software and programs I tend to use are:

  • PhotoShop
  • Illustrator
  • InDesign
  • Adobe XD
  • Sketch
  • InVision
  • Omnigraffle

During the prototyping and wireframing step, I start to seek feedback from project managers, designers and especially other developers (back-end and front-end). I find it crucial to explore solutions with as many teammates as possible.

Objectives: Page Layout, Communicate tech solutions, reveal/solve any issues
